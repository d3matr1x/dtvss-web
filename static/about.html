Dynamic Temporal Vulnerability Scoring System

DTVSS is a patent-pending, healthcare-focused extension to vulnerability scoring that models cybersecurity risk dynamically by integrating exploitability, real-time exploitation likelihood, and patient harm potential into a single multiplicative framework.

The Problem

Static vulnerability scoring systems like CVSS assign the same severity to a compromised printer and a compromised infusion pump. They measure technical severity, not clinical impact. A "Medium" on a pacemaker gets deprioritised while a "High" on a workstation gets patched first.

Meanwhile, the attack surface has moved onto the patient's body. Insulin pumps communicate over Bluetooth. Pacemakers transmit to cloud platforms. Wearable CGMs stream data through smartphones 24 hours a day. The risk is no longer static. The scoring should not be either.

The Approach

DTVSS replaces the additive, static model with a multiplicative architecture where exploitability, patient harm, and exploitation likelihood are coupled - not summed.

DTVSS(t) = (B/10 × H/10 × (1 + 15 × L(t))) × 10

B - CVSS exploitability sub-score (8.22 x AV x AC x PR x UI), supporting CVSS v2.0 through v4.0 (NVD API v2, MITRE CVE.org fallback)
H - Patient harm modifier from medical device regulatory classification (TGA / Medsafe / EU MDR / MHRA / FDA)
L(t) - Daily exploitation probability (EPSS API from FIRST.org)
KEV override - Confirmed active exploitation (CISA KEV catalog) forces score to 10.0
k = 15 - Empirically calibrated temporal amplification constant

B is computed per the CVSS v3.1 Specification (FIRST.org). For CVSS v4.0 vectors, the v3.1-compatible AV/AC/PR/UI components are extracted with AT mapped to AC equivalent. For v2.0, the exploitabilityScore is used directly from NVD.

Calibration

The system was calibrated against 96 real-world medical device CVEs spanning infusion pumps, insulin pumps, implantable cardiac devices (pacemakers, ICDs, CRT), ventilators, patient monitors, and continuous glucose monitors. The temporal amplification constant k=15 was selected to satisfy four clinical discrimination criteria ensuring that the scoring correctly differentiates between devices with different patient harm profiles and exploitation contexts.

Standards Alignment

ISO 14971 defines risk as the combination of probability of harm and severity of harm. DTVSS operationalises this definition in a cybersecurity context - continuously, computationally, and reproducibly across jurisdictions.

The system aligns with risks identified in the CSA/SANS "AI Vulnerability Storm" briefing (April 2026), including outdated risk models (Risk #5), continuous vulnerability management maturity gaps (Risk #9), and threat detection dependent on lagging intelligence (Risk #10).

Data Sources

All inputs are drawn from public, independently verifiable data sources. No proprietary data. No operator configuration. Fully deterministic and reproducible across independent implementations.

CVE discovery (CISA ICSMA pipeline)
CISA CSAF JSON (historical) + ICSMA RSS (hourly)

All CVE discovery comes from CISA ICS Medical Advisories - the authoritative public record of medical device cybersecurity vulnerabilities. CSAF JSON provides the full historical archive of structured advisories from the official CISA GitHub repository. The ICSMA RSS feed provides hourly ingestion of new advisories. NVD is used only for CVSS enrichment (scoring), not for CVE discovery, preventing false positives from keyword search. CVEs with ICSMA advisories are tagged with a clickable ICS-CERT -> indicator linking directly to the advisory.

Scoring inputs
B - NVD API v2 -> MITRE CVE.org -> CISA Vulnrichment L(t) - EPSS API (FIRST.org) KEV - CISA KEV Catalog H - TGA / Medsafe / EU MDR / MHRA / FDA

Why This Matters Now

On 15 April 2026, NIST announced fundamental changes to NVD operations. CVE submissions increased 263% between 2020 and 2025, and submissions in Q1 2026 were a further third higher than the same period last year. Despite enriching 42,000 CVEs in 2025 - 45% more than any prior year - NIST can no longer keep pace.

The signals were visible years earlier. In February 2024, NVD enrichment slowed sharply after NIST funding was cut by 12%. By May 2024, over 93% of new vulnerabilities remained unanalysed - including more than half of all known exploited vulnerabilities. NIST contracted Analygence for $865,000 to clear the backlog, but by September 2024 the unenriched count had grown to over 18,000 CVEs - 72% of all reported vulnerabilities. In April 2025, the entire CVE programme temporarily lost funding. The backlog was never cleared.

Under the new model, NIST will only enrich CVEs that appear in the CISA KEV catalog, affect federal government software, or meet the critical software definition under Executive Order 14028. All other CVEs will be listed but will not receive severity scores, product lists, or other enrichment data from NIST.

For medical device cybersecurity, this creates a critical blind spot. Most medical device CVEs are not in the KEV catalog and do not affect federal software. An unenriched CVE for a networked infusion pump or implantable cardiac device will have no severity score, no product list, and no actionable metadata from NVD. Clinical engineering teams relying solely on NVD for vulnerability prioritisation will not see these risks until it is too late.

This is the exact gap DTVSS was designed to address. The DTVSS data pipeline does not depend on any single source. The manufacturer and CVE index is built exclusively from CISA ICS Medical Advisories - CSAF JSON for the full historical archive and the ICSMA RSS feed for hourly ingestion. NVD is used only for CVSS enrichment when CISA's structured data lacks a severity score, with fallback to the MITRE CVE.org API and CISA Vulnrichment ADP container. EPSS provides temporal exploitation likelihood regardless of NVD status. The KEV catalog provides confirmed exploitation data independently.

The multiplicative architecture - coupling exploitability, patient harm, and exploitation likelihood - was designed precisely because static, centralised scoring cannot scale. DTVSS computes risk continuously from distributed public sources, with no dependency on any single enrichment pipeline.
