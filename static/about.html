Dynamic Temporal Vulnerability Scoring System

DTVSS is a patent-pending, healthcare-focused extension to vulnerability scoring that models cybersecurity risk dynamically by integrating exploitability, real-time exploitation likelihood, and patient harm potential into a single multiplicative framework.

The Problem

Static vulnerability scoring systems like CVSS assign the same severity to a compromised printer and a compromised infusion pump. They measure technical severity, not clinical impact. A "Medium" on a pacemaker gets deprioritised while a "High" on a workstation gets patched first.

Meanwhile, the attack surface has moved onto the patient's body. Insulin pumps communicate over Bluetooth. Pacemakers transmit to cloud platforms. Wearable CGMs stream data through smartphones 24 hours a day. The risk is no longer static. The scoring should not be either.

The Approach

DTVSS replaces the additive, static model with a multiplicative architecture where exploitability, patient harm, and exploitation likelihood are coupled — not summed.

DTVSS(t) = (B/10 × H/10 × (1 + 15 × L(t))) × 10

B — CVSS exploitability sub-score, supporting CVSS v2.0 through v4.0 (NVD API v2, MITRE CVE.org fallback)
H — Patient harm modifier from medical device regulatory classification (TGA / Medsafe / EU MDR / MHRA / FDA)
L(t) — Daily exploitation probability (EPSS API from FIRST.org)
KEV override — Confirmed active exploitation (CISA KEV catalog) forces score to 10.0
k = 15 — Empirically calibrated temporal amplification constant

Calibration

The system was calibrated against 96 real-world medical device CVEs spanning infusion pumps, insulin pumps, implantable cardiac devices (pacemakers, ICDs, CRT), ventilators, patient monitors, and continuous glucose monitors. The temporal amplification constant k=15 was selected to satisfy four clinical discrimination criteria ensuring that the scoring correctly differentiates between devices with different patient harm profiles and exploitation contexts.

Standards Alignment

ISO 14971 defines risk as the combination of probability of harm and severity of harm. DTVSS operationalises this definition in a cybersecurity context — continuously, computationally, and reproducibly across jurisdictions.

The system aligns with risks identified in the CSA/SANS "AI Vulnerability Storm" briefing (April 2026), including outdated risk models (Risk #5), continuous vulnerability management maturity gaps (Risk #9), and threat detection dependent on lagging intelligence (Risk #10).

Data Sources

All inputs are drawn from public, independently verifiable data sources. No proprietary data. No operator configuration. Fully deterministic and reproducible across independent implementations.
